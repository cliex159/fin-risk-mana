8.1 Problem 1

The prices and dividends of a stock are given as follows.

time \(P_t\) \(D_t\)
1 52 0.2
2 54 0.2
3 53 0.2
4 59 0.25
  1. Determine \(R_2\) and \(R_4(3)\).

\[\begin{align*} R_2&=\frac{P_2-P_1+d_2}{P_1}=0.042 \\ R_3&=\frac{P_3-P_2+d_3}{P_2}=-0.015 \\ R_4&=\frac{P_4-P_3+d_4}{P_3}=0.118 \\ \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} R_4(3)&=(1+R_4)(1+R_3)(1+R_2)-1 \\ R_4(3)&=(1+0.118)(1+-0.015)(1+0.042)-1 \\ &\approx 0.148 \end{align*}\]

Answer: \(R_2 \approx 0.042\) and \(R_4(3) \approx 0.148\).

  1. Determine \(r_3\).

\[\begin{align*} r_3&=\ln(1+R_3) \\ &\approx R_3 \\ &\approx-0.015 \end{align*}\]

Answer: \(r_3 \approx -0.015\)

8.2 Problem 2

Assume that the log returns \(r_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0.06, 0.47)\) are i.i.d.

  1. Determine the distribution of \(r_t(4)\).

\[\begin{align*} r_t(4)&=r_t+r_{t-1}+r_{t-2}+r_{t-3} \\ &\sim 4 \times \mathcal{N}(0.06,0.47) \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(4 \times 0.06,4 \times 0.47) \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(0.24,1.88) \end{align*}\]

Answer: The distribution of \(r_t(4)\) is \(\mathcal{N}(0.24,1.88)\).

  1. Find \(cov(r_2(1), r_2(2))\).

\[\begin{align*} cov(r_2(1),r_2(2))&=cov(r_2,r_2+r_1) \\ &=cov(r_2,r_2)+cov(r_2,r_1) \\ &=var(r_2)+cov(r_1,r_2) \\ &=0.47 \end{align*}\]

Answer: \(cov(r_2(1),r_2(2))=0.47\)

  1. Determine the distribution of \(r_t(3)\) if \(r_{tâˆ’2} = 0.6\).

\[\begin{align*} [r_t(3)|r_{t-2}=0.6]&=[r_t+r_{t-1}+r_{t-2}|r_{t-2}=0.6] \\ &=r_t+r_{t-1}+0.6 \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(0.06,0.47)+\mathcal{N}(0.06,0.47)+0.6 \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(0.72,0.94) \end{align*}\]

Answer: If \(r_{t-2}=0.6\), the distribution of \(r_t(3)\) is \(\mathcal{N}(0.72,0.94)\).

8.3 Problem 3

Assume a stock of current price $97 with i.i.d. log returns

\[r_t \sim \mathcal{N}(2 \times 10^{âˆ’4}, 9 \times 10^{âˆ’4})\] What is the probability that its price exceeds \(\$100\) after 20 trading days?

\[\begin{align*} \ln \left( \frac{P_{20}}{P_0} \right) &= r_{20}(20) \\ &= \sum_{t=1}^{20}r_t \\ &\sim 20 \times \mathcal{N}(2 \times 10^{âˆ’4}, 9 \times 10^{âˆ’4}) \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(20 \times 2 \times 10^{âˆ’4}, 20 \times 9 \times 10^{âˆ’4}) \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(0.004, 0.018) \\ \\ \rightarrow \ln(P_{20})&=\ln(P_{0})+r_{20}(20) \\ &\sim \ln(97)+\mathcal{N}(0.004, 0.018) \\ &\sim \mathcal{N}(4.579, 0.018) \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \mathcal{P}(P_{20}>100)&=\mathcal{P}(\ln(P_{20})>\ln(100)) \\ &=\mathcal{P} \left(\frac{\ln(P_{20})-4.579}{\sqrt{0.018}} > \frac{\ln(100)-4.579}{\sqrt{0.018}} \right) \\ &=\mathcal{P} \left(\mathcal{Z} > \frac{\ln(100)-4.579}{\sqrt{0.018}} \right) \\ &=\mathcal{P} \left(\mathcal{Z} > 0.195 \right) \\ &=\mathcal{P} \left(\mathcal{Z} < -0.195 \right) \\ &=0.423 \end{align*}\]

Answer: The probability that its price exceeds \(\$100\) after 20 trading days is 42.3%.
